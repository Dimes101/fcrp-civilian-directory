Become a Civilian

Training Process & Requirements
Getting Started
Training Process
1
Complete the Examination
Go to #examination-submissions in the Civilian Discord. Check the pinned message and open the official FCRP Examination form. The exam is open-book. Submit and wait to be contacted about your results.
2
Complete CAD & TeamSpeak Setup
Go to #cad-setup-submissions. Read the pinned message, complete all steps (TeamSpeak first), then submit the form. Wait to be contacted by the Civilian Staff Team.
3
Begin Patrolling as Probationary Civilian
Once onboarded you start as a Probationary Civilian with Level 1 Contact. Log all patrols within 48 hours. Full-time: 10 hrs/cycle. Reserve: 2 hrs/cycle.
4
Work Towards Promotion
Promotions are based on activity, performance, and professionalism. Senior Civilians may apply for the Chain of Command. Review the Activity & Promotion Guidelines for full criteria.
Transfer PolicyFull-time civilians are eligible for transfer after 10 days and 10 hours logged in-server, with good standing with CoC. Returning members do not bypass this requirement.

Judicial Services

Legal Roleplay Division

About Judicial Services

The Florida Judicial Services is a sub-department within the FCRP Civilian Department focusing on legal professional RP — lawyers, judges, court clerks, and bailiffs. Responsible for all legal RP including court cases, plea bargains, and legal investigations. Works closely with Law Enforcement for realistic and fair outcomes.

Businesses & Organizations

Player-Run Enterprises

About Businesses & Organizations

Any Civilian I+ member may propose a business or organization. If approved, the civilian must develop a Roster, SOP, and Business Plan. Active engagement is required to maintain approval and access perks.

Requirements to Propose

• Must be Civilian I or higher

• Submit proposal to Civilian CoC

• Provide: Roster, SOP, and Business Plan

• Must remain actively engaged once approved

Business Perks

Active businesses unlock special server perks and Tebex rewards. See the Business Perks document for the full list of available benefits.

Civilian Conduct

In-Game & Out-of-Game Standards
Callsign PolicyAll members must display their callsign on all platforms at all times: First L. Civ-###
In-Game Rules
Prohibited Conduct
FailRP

Unrealistic actions or breaking immersion — refusing injury RP, pulling weapons from nowhere, using overpowered abilities, or metagaming.

ForceRP

Forcing another player to roleplay a certain action without their consent. All RP must respect player autonomy.

Metagaming

Using out-of-character knowledge to influence in-character decisions. All RP communication must remain in-game.

Combat Logging

Intentionally leaving the server to avoid RP outcomes such as arrest, jail, or medical treatment. Strictly prohibited.

Cop Baiting

Intentionally provoking a law enforcement response — repeatedly speeding past officers or engaging in disruptive behavior to get their attention.

Leeching

Drawing attention away from an ongoing RP scene or LEO operation. Only allowed with explicit consent of all involved parties.

VMenu Abuse

Misusing server features such as teleport or self-healing outside of valid RP. The VMenu must never be abused in any way.

RDM

Killing any player without a valid roleplay reason. Interactions with locals are treated the same as interactions with players.

Banned Roleplay ScenariosSexual RP or nudity of any nature · Federal government or presidential RP without Admin approval · Any explicit scenarios involving minors
Suicide & Self-Harm RoleplayRequires advance approval from Civilian Staff or Head Administration. Must be story-driven, involve appropriate LEO resources, and use proper scripts. No graphic depictions permitted at any time.
Disciplinary Action
Levels of Discipline
I
Verbal Warning
For minor issues not severe enough to warrant a written record.
II
10-90 Report
Written report filed for more serious violations requiring a formal record.
III
10-93 Suspension
Temporary removal from duty for severe violations.
IV
Re-Training
Required when behavior or performance is below standard and additional training is needed.
V
Termination
Final resort for unacceptable conduct that cannot be rectified through other means.
